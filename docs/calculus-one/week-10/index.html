Newton’s Method

  • Also called the Newton-Raphson method
  • To solve the equation of the form f(x)=0f(x) = 0f(x)=0, so the roots of the equation(方程的根) correspond to the x-intercepts of the graph of fff. The root that we are trying to find is labeled rrr in the figure.
    • We start with a first approximation x1x_1x1​ , which is obtained by guessing,
    • Consider the tangent line L to the curve y=f(x)y = f(x)y=f(x) at the point (x1,f(x1))(x_1, f(x_1))(x1​,f(x1​)) and look at the x-intercept of L, labeled x2x_2x2​.
    • The idea behind Newton’s method is that the tangent line is close to the curve and so its x-intercept, x2x_2x2​, is close to the x-intercept of the curve (namely, the root rrr that we are seeking). Because the tangent is a line, we can easily find its x-intercept.
    • To find a formula for x2x_2x2​ in terms of x1x_1x1​ we use the fact that the slope of L is f′(x1)f'(x_1)f′(x1​), so its equation is:
      • y−f(x1)=f′(x1)(x−x1)y - f(x_1) = f'(x_1)(x - x_1)y−f(x1​)=f′(x1​)(x−x1​)
    • Since the x-intercept of L is x2x_2x2​, we know that point (x2,0x_2, 0x2​,0) is on the line, and so:
      • 0−f(x1)=f′(x1)(x2−x1)0 - f(x_1) = f'(x_1)(x_2 - x_1)0−f(x1​)=f′(x1​)(x2​−x1​)
    • If f′(x1)≠0f'(x_1) \ne 0f′(x1​)=0, we can solve this equation for x2x_2x2​:
      • x2=x1−f(x1)f′(x1)x_2 = x_1 - \frac{f(x_1)}{f'(x_1)}x2​=x1​−f′(x1​)f(x1​)​
      • Then x3=x2−f(x2)f′(x2)x_3 = x_2 - \frac{f(x_2)}{f'(x_2)}x3​=x2​−f′(x2​)f(x2​)​
    • If we keep repeating this process, we obtain a sequence of approximations x1,x2,x3,x4,…x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4, \dotsx1​,x2​,x3​,x4​,… as shown:
    • In general, if the nnnth approximation is xnx_nxn​ and f′(xn)≠0f'(x_n) \ne 0f′(xn​)=0, then the next approximation is given by:
      • xn+1=xn−f(xn)f′(xn)x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}xn+1​=xn​−f′(xn​)f(xn​)​
    • If the number xnx_nxn​ become closer and closer to rrr as nnn becomes large, then we say that the sequence converges to rrr and we write $$\lim_{n \to \infty}x_n = r$$

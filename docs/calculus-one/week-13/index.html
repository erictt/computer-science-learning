Week 13 - Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus is appropriately named because it establishes a connection between the two branches of calculus: differential calculus and integral calculus.

Theorem

  • Suppose f:[a,b]→Rf: [a, b] \to \mathbb{R}f:[a,b]→R is continuous. Let FFF be the accumulation function, given by $$F(x) = \int_a^x f(t) dt$$. Then FFF is continuous on [a,b][a, b][a,b], differentiable on (a, b), and F′(x)=f(x)F'(x) = f(x)F′(x)=f(x).

    • To prove: $$F’(x) = \frac{F(x+h) - F(x)}{h} \approx \frac{f(x) \cdot h}{h} = f(x)$$

  • Supposef:[a,b]→Rf: [a, b] \to \mathbb{R}f:[a,b]→R is continuous, and FFF is an antidirevative of fff. Then $$\int_a^b f(x) dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

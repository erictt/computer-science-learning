Orthogonal Vectors & Orthogonal Spaces

  • Vectors x and y are considered to be orthogonal (perpendicular) if they meet at a right angle: $$x^T y = 0$$

Normal Vector

  • The normal vector, often simply called the “normal,” to a surface is a vector which is perpendicular to the surface at a given point.
  • For example:
    • Define the plane as format: Ax+By+Cz=DAx + By + Cz = DAx+By+Cz=D

Cross Product

  • the cross product or vector product is a binary operation on two vectors in three-dimensional space (R3\mathbb{R}^3R3) and is denoted by the symbol ×.
    • [a0a1a2]×[b0b1b2]=[a1b2−a2b1a2b0−a0b2a0b1−a1b0]\begin{bmatrix} a_0 \\ a_1 \\ a_2 \end{bmatrix} \times \begin{bmatrix} b_0 \\ b_1 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1 \\ a_2 b_0 - a_0 b_2 \\ a_0 b_1 - a_1 b_0 \end{bmatrix}⎣⎡​a0​a1​a2​​⎦⎤​×⎣⎡​b0​b1​b2​​⎦⎤​=⎣⎡​a1​b2​−a2​b1​a2​b0​−a0​b2​a0​b1​−a1​b0​​⎦⎤​
  • Given two linearly independent vectors a and b, the cross product, a × b, is a vector that is perpendicular to both a and b and thus normal to the plane containing them.
    • Because [a1b2−a2b1a2b0−a0b2a0b1−a1b0]T[a0a1a2]=0\begin{bmatrix} a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1 \\ a_2 b_0 - a_0 b_2 \\ a_0 b_1 - a_1 b_0 \end{bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} a_0 \\ a_1 \\ a_2 \end{bmatrix} = 0⎣⎡​a1​b2​−a2​b1​a2​b0​−a0​b2​a0​b1​−a1​b0​​⎦⎤​T⎣⎡​a0​a1​a2​​⎦⎤​=0 and [a1b2−a2b1a2b0−a0b2a0b1−a1b0]T[b0b1b2]=0\begin{bmatrix} a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1 \\ a_2 b_0 - a_0 b_2 \\ a_0 b_1 - a_1 b_0 \end{bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} b_0 \\ b_1 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix} = 0⎣⎡​a1​b2​−a2​b1​a2​b0​−a0​b2​a0​b1​−a1​b0​​⎦⎤​T⎣⎡​b0​b1​b2​​⎦⎤​=0
  • So we can use vectors a and b to get n. (n=a×bn = a \times bn=a×b)

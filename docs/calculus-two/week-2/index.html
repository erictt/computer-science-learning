Week 2 - Series

Definition

  • In general, if we try to add the terms of an infinite sequence {an}n=1∞\{a_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}{an​}n=1∞​ we get an expression of the form $$a_1+a_2+a_3+\cdots+a_n+\cdots$$
  • which is called an infinite series (or just a series) and is denoted, for short, by the symbol $$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \ \text{or}\ \sum{a_n}$$

Convergent and Divergent

  • Given a series ∑n=1∞an=a1+a2+a3+⋯\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots∑n=1∞​an​=a1​+a2​+a3​+⋯, let sns_nsn​ denote its nth partial sum: $$s_n = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots + a_n$$
  • If the sequence {sn}\{s_n\}{sn​} is convergent and lim⁡n→∞sn=s\lim_{n \to \infty} s_n = slimn→∞​sn​=s exists as a real number, then the series ∑an\sum a_n∑an​ is called convergent and we write $$a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots + a_n + \cdots = s \ \text{or}\ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n = s$$
  • The number s is called the sum of the series. If the sequence {sn}\{s_n\}{sn​} is divergent, then the series is called divergent.

Geometric Series

  • a+ar+ar2+ar3+⋯+arn−1+⋯=∑n=1∞arn−1, a≠0a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \cdots + ar^{n-1} + \cdots = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} ar^{n-1}, \ a \ne 0a+ar+ar2+ar3+⋯+arn−1+⋯=∑n=1∞​arn−1, a​=0
  • Each term is obtained from the preceding one by multiplying it by the common ratio r.
  • If r=1r = 1r=1, then sn=a+a+a+⋯+a=na→±∞s_n = a + a + a + \cdots + a = na \to \pm \inftysn​=a+a+a+⋯+a=na→±∞. Since lim⁡n→∞sn\lim_{n \to \infty} s_nlimn→∞​sn​ doesn’t exist, the geometric series diverges in this case.
  • If r≠1r \ne 1r​=1, we have $$\begin{aligned} s_n &= a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \cdots + ar^{n-1} \
    rs_n &= ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \cdots + ar^{n}

Telescoping Series

  • A telescoping series is a series whose partial sums eventually only have a fixed number of terms after cancellation.
  • For example, the series $$\sum _{n=1}^{\infty }{\frac {1}{n(n+1)}}$$
    • (the series of reciprocals of pronic numbers) simplifies as $${\begin{aligned}\sum _{n=1}^{\infty }{\frac {1}{n(n+1)}}&{}=\sum _{n=1}^{\infty }\left({\frac {1}{n}}-{\frac {1}{n+1}}\right)\{}&{}=\lim _{N\to \infty }\sum _{n=1}^{N}\left({\frac {1}{n}}-{\frac {1}{n+1}}\right)\{}&{}=\lim _{N\to \infty }\left\lbrack {\left(1-{\frac {1}{2}}\right)+\left({\frac {1}{2}}-{\frac {1}{3}}\right)+\cdots +\left({\frac {1}{N}}-{\frac {1}{N+1}}\right)}\right\rbrack \{}&{}=\lim _{N\to \infty }\left\lbrack {1+\left(-{\frac {1}{2}}+{\frac {1}{2}}\right)+\left(-{\frac {1}{3}}+{\frac {1}{3}}\right)+\cdots +\left(-{\frac {1}{N}}+{\frac {1}{N}}\right)-{\frac {1}{N+1}}}\right\rbrack \{}&{}=\lim _{N\to \infty }\left\lbrack {1-{\frac {1}{N+1}}}\right\rbrack =1.\end{aligned}}$$
